Computer Vision ML:
From Pixels to Production

9 lessons for the engineer who wants to build, train, and deploy object detection and classification systems.

You already know how to code — you can write Python, you understand basic ML concepts. But have you ever wondered how to take a raw dataset and turn it into a real-time object detector running on an edge device? Why do some models run at 30 FPS on an Orin NX while others struggle? How do you go from training in the cloud to deployment on a Qualcomm QCS8550 NPU? That's what we're here for. Not just theory — practical implementation.

What You'll Learn

  • How to design effective computer vision pipelines for object detection, classification, and tracking
  • Best practices for data collection, annotation, and augmentation
  • How to select and modify model architectures (YOLO, SSD, EfficientDet, Transformers) for your specific use case
  • PyTorch fundamentals for training computer vision models
  • Model optimization techniques including quantization, pruning, and tensorRT
  • Deployment strategies for embedded systems like NVIDIA Orin NX and Qualcomm QCS8550
  • Real-time inference optimization and object tracking algorithms
  • ONNX conversion and cross-platform deployment

How It Works

Each lesson combines clear explanations with interactive widgets you can play with — configurable model visualizers, training simulators, deployment configurators. Everything runs in your browser, so there's nothing to install. The course is mobile-first and works great on your phone or laptop.

Lessons build on each other, so start from Lesson 1 and work your way through. Mark each one complete as you go — your progress is saved in your browser.
