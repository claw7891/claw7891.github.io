Model Architecture Selection

Choosing the Right Model
Balancing Accuracy, Speed, and Size

Understanding the trade-offs between different CV architectures

You've collected and annotated your data, but now you're staring at a zoo of model names: YOLOv8, SSD, EfficientDet, Faster R-CNN, DETR, ViT... How do you choose? In this lesson, we'll learn how to pick the right architecture for your specific constraints and goals.

Factors to Consider

When selecting a model architecture, consider these key factors:

  • Task Type: Detection, classification, segmentation, etc. require different architectures
  • Accuracy Requirements: How precise do your predictions need to be?
  • Speed Requirements: Do you need real-time processing (e.g., 30 FPS)?
  • Resource Constraints: What are the memory, power, and compute limits of your target hardware?
  • Model Size: How much storage and RAM can you allocate?
  • Development Complexity: How much time do you have for implementation and tuning?

Popular Architecture Families

Here are the main families of computer vision models you'll encounter:

Two-Stage Detectors

Examples: Faster R-CNN, Mask R-CNN
Pros: High accuracy
Cons: Slow, computationally expensive

One-Stage Detectors

Examples: YOLO, SSD, RetinaNet
Pros: Fast, good for real-time
Cons: Lower accuracy on small objects

Transformer-Based Models

Examples: DETR, ViT, Swin Transformer
Pros: State-of-the-art accuracy, flexible
Cons: Computationally heavy, longer training

Efficient Models

Examples: MobileNet, EfficientNet, EfficientDet
Pros: Designed for mobile/embedded, good accuracy/speed trade-off
Cons: May require specific hardware optimizations

Embedded-Specific Considerations

When targeting embedded systems like Orin NX or QCS8550, consider:

  1. Hardware Acceleration: Does the model work well with GPU, NPU, or DSP accelerators?
  2. Memory Bandwidth: Models that access memory randomly may suffer on embedded systems
  3. Power Efficiency: Some architectures consume more power than others for the same accuracy
  4. Quantization Friendliness: How well does the model maintain accuracy after quantization?

Quick Start Recommendations

For common embedded CV tasks:

  • Real-time Object Detection: YOLOv8n or YOLOv8s (for speed) or YOLOv8m (for balance)
  • High-Accuracy Detection: EfficientDet-D0/D1 or YOLOv8l
  • Classification: MobileNetV3 or EfficientNet-B0
  • Segmentation: DeepLabV3+ with MobileNet backbone or YOLOv8-seg
